Introduction

The Clonal Selection Algorithm is inspired by the biological immune system, where antibodies are selected, cloned, and mutated to fight antigens.

Concept
Antibodies → candidate solutions
Antigen → problem
Fitness → solution quality
Working
Initialize a population of random solutions
Evaluate fitness of each solution
Select best solutions
Clone selected solutions
Apply mutation to clones
Replace weak solutions with improved ones
Repeat for multiple generations
Observation
Fitness improves over generations
Best solution gradually approaches optimum
Advantages
Simple and effective
Good exploration capability
Avoids local minima
Conclusion

The Clonal Selection Algorithm successfully optimizes the solution by iterative cloning and mutation, mimicking the biological immune response.
