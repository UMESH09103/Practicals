Introduction

Artificial Immune Systems (AIS) are inspired by the biological immune system and are used for pattern recognition and classification tasks.

Concept
Antigens → Input patterns
Antibodies → Classifier
Affinity → Matching quality
Working
Input dataset representing structural features is generated
Data is split into training and testing sets
A classifier is trained (simulating immune memory)
The model predicts whether structure is damaged or not
Accuracy is calculated
Application

Used in:

Structural health monitoring
Damage detection in buildings and machines
Advantages
Robust to noise
Good pattern recognition
Adaptive learning
Conclusion

The artificial immune system successfully classifies structural damage patterns, demonstrating its effectiveness in pattern recognition tasks.
